Stop caching of certain files!

A common annoyance for new developers is caching - when you browser stores static files (css, images, js, etc..) to speed up load times of new pages. It is this caching that causes changes to these files not be be seen when you refresh you page (I am sure at some stage you have/will experience this).
A super handy trick to remember is the "get args" method - sound complicated? It really isn't! Add a get argument at the end of your href or src and it will always think you are loading a different file - which will mean you always get the most up to data version! EG. href="style.css" becomes href="style.css?foo=bar" or href="style.css?abc=123" or maybe even href="style.css?stephen=awesome"

Do remember to take remove the get argument before deploying though!
